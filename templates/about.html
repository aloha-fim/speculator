Motivation

“I found the perfect apartment …and I want to find others like it.”


Go on realtor websites and spend an exhausting search looking through pictures. Realize your ONLY capability is limited filtered parameters such as number of beds, baths, etc.

So how do you define “give me a condo that I like” in your search?


Goals

We offer a product for customers to search for their perfect apartment through image similarity using neural networks.


The ideal apartment can be found in passing through a real estate website, social media, or an open house. However, our product takes the next step to recommend condos with shared similar features.


Discovering features from pattern recognition of color, shapes, and dimensions allow us to capture the subjective criteria of the apartment.


We intend to find the “feeling” determined by a customer, which is very difficult to meaningfully describe. Hence, our goal is to answer the question, “can you provide me listings based on what I feel is important?”

Design

Initial Proposal Nothing

Initially we had the application implementation shown on the cover page.



Final Design Final Version - Alter Design to Human Psychology

We pulled a script on the periodic table of elements from the web and adjusted the design to reflect the human reaction of viewing a web site, generally starting from the top left corner and ending in the direction at the bottom right corner. Hence, we take advantage of this pattern for the user to focus attention on the headline “Boston Condo Connections” and then the start button.

We also chose a visually appealing color palette of blue to influence the user with a tranquil psychological impact. Color choice is an important factor to branding with examples learned of sports sites with black outlines to represent power and strength and health sites with shades of orange to promote optimism.

Three of the largest real estate sites (zillow, realtor.com, trulia) all have white backgrounds to show cleanliness and safety, in line with representing images of potential homes. As a result, we transitioned from a black background on the cover page to represent the power and strength of machine learning by the CNN and followed suit in color scheme with industry norm to the other pages of our application.

Initial Proposal Train our own Model

There was an initial push for a CNN to take an image and adjust the feature training dealing with convolutions, pooling, and otherwise changing the layers to the neural network. Moreover, the classification to the CNN would include weighting labels for rooms such as a kitchen by countertop, stove, refrigerator, etc.

Given the time frame of the project, Jasmine and Michelle recommended that we not train our own network. This was salient advice as our first prediction resulted in accuracy of less than 20%. Even if adjustments weights to labels were optimized and accuracy improved, critical factors would have been excluded such as ceiling height, floor type, open windows, etc.



Final Design Pretained CNN

Ultimately, we decided to use Keras and the pretrained model of VGG16. In addition, the prediction method of the pre-traimed model uses an algorithm in the numpy library to match similar objects by euclidian distance. The lesson learned that is more training data is required for more training iterations and weight updates, in order to make better predictions in the network.

Initial Proposal Normalized Database

The initial proposal was for designing a normalized database schema.



Final Design Keep it Simple

Given that the project scope allowed for 4 tables to efficiently run the application, there was no need to over-engineer the project and branching out the tables any further.
